📘 Unit 1 of 8

Hello! Nice to Meet You

Learn to greet people, introduce yourself, say where you are from, and use the verb "to be." Practice with audio, dialogues, and writing activities.

A1. Vocabulary

Essential Vocabulary

Greetings

Hello / Hi
A common greeting
"Hello! How are you?"
Good morning
A greeting used before 12 PM
"Good morning, teacher!"
Good afternoon
A greeting used after 12 PM
"Good afternoon, class."
Good evening
A greeting used in the evening
"Good evening, Mr. Smith."
Goodbye / Bye
Said when leaving
"Goodbye! See you tomorrow."
Nice to meet you
Said when you meet someone for the first time
"I am Anna. Nice to meet you."

Personal Information

Name
What people call you
"My name is Carlos."
Country
Where you are from
"I am from Argentina."
Age
How old you are
"I am twenty-five years old."
Job / Occupation
What you do for work
"I am a student."
B1. Grammar

Verb "To Be" — Present Simple

I am → I'm a teacher.
You are → You're my friend.
He is / She is / It is → She's from Brazil.
We are → We're students.
They are → They're happy.
Questions: Are you a student? Is she from Mexico?
Negatives: I am not (I'm not) tired. He is not (isn't) a doctor.
C1. Key Collocations

Useful Expressions for Introductions

Nice to meet you
Used when you meet someone new
"Hi, I'm Tom. Nice to meet you."
Where are you from?
Asking about someone's country or city
"Where are you from? I'm from Spain."
How are you?
Asking how someone feels
"How are you? I'm fine, thank you."
What do you do?
Asking about someone's job
"What do you do? I'm a nurse."

🎯 Practice Sentence:

"Hello, my name is Maria. I am from Argentina. I am a student. Nice to meet you!"

My Daily Routine

Learn to describe your daily activities, tell the time, and use the Present Simple tense with everyday verbs.

A3. Vocabulary

Daily Activities

Wake up
To stop sleeping
"I wake up at seven o'clock."
Have breakfast
To eat the first meal of the day
"I have breakfast at half past seven."
Go to work / school
To travel to your job or school
"I go to work by bus."
Have lunch
To eat the midday meal
"I have lunch at twelve thirty."
Come home
To return to your house
"I come home at five o'clock."
Have dinner
To eat the evening meal
"We have dinner at eight o'clock."
Take a shower
To wash your body with water
"I take a shower every morning."
Go to bed
To go to sleep
"I go to bed at ten thirty."

Telling the Time

O'clock
"It is three o'clock." (3:00)
Half past
"It is half past six." (6:30)
Quarter past
"It is quarter past nine." (9:15)
Quarter to
"It is quarter to eight." (7:45)
B3. Grammar

Present Simple — Daily Routines

I / You / We / They → I get up at seven. They eat lunch at noon.
He / She / It → She gets up at six. He eats lunch at one.
Negatives: I don't work on Sundays. She doesn't drive to work.
Questions: Do you work on Saturdays? Does he take the bus?

Juice

Milk

B4. Grammar

Countable & Uncountable Nouns — Some / Any

Countable: an apple, two eggs, three tomatoes
Uncountable: water, rice, bread, milk, cheese
Some (positive): There is some milk. I have some apples.
Any (negative & questions): Is there any bread? There isn't any juice.
A / An: I want an apple. She eats a sandwich.
How much / How many: How much water? How many eggs?

B5. Grammar

There is / There are — Prepositions of Place

There is (singular): There is a lamp on the table.
There are (plural): There are two bedrooms in my house.
Negative: There isn't a garage. There aren't any pictures.
Questions: Is there a garden? Are there any chairs?
Prepositions:
on (on the table), in (in the box), under (under the bed),
next to (next to the window), between (between the chairs), in front of (in front of the sofa)

B7. Grammar

Present Continuous — What Are You Wearing?

Questions: What are you wearing? Is she wearing a hat?
Negatives: I'm not wearing shoes. He isn't wearing a jacket.
Present Continuous = NOW: I am sitting. She is reading. They are talking.

B8. Grammar

Like / Don't Like + Adverbs of Frequency

Like + verb-ing: I like swimming. She likes cooking. They like reading.
Don't like: I don't like running. He doesn't like dancing.
Love / Hate: I love playing soccer. She hates getting up early.
Questions: Do you like swimming? Does he like cooking?
Adverbs of Frequency:
always (100%) → usually (80%) → often (60%) → sometimes (40%) → rarely (20%) → never (0%)

I always listen to music. She sometimes plays tennis. He never watches horror movies.
