📘 Unit 1 of 8

Hello! Nice to Meet You

Learn to greet people, introduce yourself, say where you are from, and use the verb "to be." Practice with audio, dialogues, and writing activities.

📚 Vocabulary

Essential Vocabulary

Greetings

Hello / Hi
A common greeting
"Hello! How are you?"
Good morning
A greeting used before 12 PM
"Good morning, teacher!"
Good afternoon
A greeting used after 12 PM
"Good afternoon, class."
Good evening
A greeting used in the evening
"Good evening, Mr. Smith."
Goodbye / Bye
Said when leaving
"Goodbye! See you tomorrow."
Nice to meet you
Said when you meet someone for the first time
"I am Anna. Nice to meet you."

Personal Information

Name
What people call you
"My name is Carlos."
Country
Where you are from
"I am from Argentina."
Age
How old you are
"I am twenty-five years old."
Job / Occupation
What you do for work
"I am a student."
📝 Grammar

Verb "To Be" — Present Simple

I am → I'm a teacher.
You are → You're my friend.
He is / She is / It is → She's from Brazil.
We are → We're students.
They are → They're happy.
Questions: Are you a student? Is she from Mexico?
Negatives: I am not (I'm not) tired. He is not (isn't) a doctor.
💫 Key Collocations

Useful Expressions for Introductions

Nice to meet you
Used when you meet someone new
"Hi, I'm Tom. Nice to meet you."
Where are you from?
Asking about someone's country or city
"Where are you from? I'm from Spain."
How are you?
Asking how someone feels
"How are you? I'm fine, thank you."
What do you do?
Asking about someone's job
"What do you do? I'm a nurse."

🎯 Practice Sentence:

"Hello, my name is Maria. I am from Argentina. I am a student. Nice to meet you!"

🎧 Listening

Listening Comprehension

🔊 Audio 1: Two People Meet at a Party

🎵 Listen to the dialogue:

📝 Transcript:

Tom: Hi! My name is Tom. What is your name?

Lucia: Hello Tom! I am Lucia. Nice to meet you!

Tom: Nice to meet you too, Lucia. Where are you from?

Lucia: I am from Italy. And you?

Tom: I am from the United States. I am from New York.

Lucia: Oh, great! What do you do, Tom?

Tom: I am a teacher. And you?

Lucia: I am a student. I study English.

🗣️ Speaking

Speaking Practice

Dialogue Practice: Meeting a New Classmate

Practice this dialogue with a partner. Listen first, then repeat.

Student A: Hello! My name is ______. What is your name?
Student B: Hi! I am ______. Nice to meet you!
Student A: Nice to meet you too. Where are you from?
Student B: I am from ______. And you?
Student A: I am from ______. How old are you?
Student B: I am ______ years old.

🎯 Speaking Task

Walk around the class and introduce yourself to three different people. Write their names, countries, and ages in your notebook.

  • Say your name
  • Ask their name
  • Ask where they are from
  • Ask how old they are
  • Say "Nice to meet you!"
📖 Reading

Reading Comprehension

Read about these people:

Hi! My name is Sarah. I am from England. I am twenty-eight years old. I am a nurse. I work at a hospital in London. I like my job very much.


Hello! I am Kenji. I am from Japan. I am thirty-three years old. I am an engineer. I live in Tokyo. I am married and I have two children.


Good morning! My name is Fatima. I am from Morocco. I am nineteen years old. I am a university student. I study medicine. I live with my family.

✍️ Writing

Writing Practice

Task: Write About Yourself

Write a short paragraph about yourself. Use the text about Sarah, Kenji, and Fatima as a model.

📝 Template:

Hello! My name is _______________.
I am from _______________.
I am _______________ years old.
I am a _______________.
I live in _______________.
I like _______________.

✏️ Extra Writing Task:

Write about a friend or a family member. Use "He is..." or "She is..." instead of "I am..."

Example: "This is my friend Pedro. He is from Mexico. He is twenty years old. He is a student."
